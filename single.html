Summer at Chinese University of HongKong

Avinash Gupta, Electrical Engineering, 2016EEB1071

How I got the internship?

The selection procedure had such an unsavoury reputation that I needed to be impervious to rejections and sent around 400-500 emails from which I got four acceptances, namely from Taiwan, France, IISc and Hong Kong. As I was determined to pursue PhD and do research in future, I chose the professor with the best profile, University and most close to my past projects which eventually ended up with me joining at CUHK as a Research Assistant. I have an average CGPA of 7.71 but had plenty of good research projects. And, going through the profile of the professor and his requirements, it felt like my email landed at the time when he was so busy that he unwarily accepted my application.

Work Experience

I am working at CUHK as a Research Assistant. It is the first time, I am experiencing a lab environment with lab mates from USA, UK, China, Korea, etc. working alongside. The vibrant atmosphere of the lab is so incentive that I never felt how the hours pass at work. On joining there, I got to know that I was the first UG he took from India as an intern thanks that IITs were not alien institutions for him. I am working on modelling of the carrier dynamics in 2-D materials based photodetectors used in optical waveguides. I was also given a chance to observe and carry the experiment on my own. The past research experiences at IIT Ropar were an immense help for me to strengthen my fundamentals. Even I received admiration and a PhD offer for my way of going through, pondering deep into the research papers and for my knowledge on the topic.

Stipend Offered

The stipend offered was HKD 11,500/pm (around INR 1 lac)

Living in HongKong

Hong Kong has positioned itself to become the world city of Asia. Its the city of skyscrapers, a city of charm, the sophistication of an international city, cultural diversity and cosmopolitan lifestyle is at the very core of Hong Kong's attractions. The government spends a good amount of its budget on research and education. Nearly all the universities in Hong Kong are ranked under 500 on QS world ranking. The professors in Hong Kong are the highest paid in the world, and the same goes with the PhD fellows, which also leaves the USA far behind. The people of Hong Kong mostly speak Cantonese, Chinese and English. So, the language will never be a problem. Hong Kong has many tourist attractions like beaches, Disneyland, Ocean Park, beautiful islands, scenic trekking and hiking locations, etc. One can enjoy the fruits here at their lowest price. There are various dishes that are worthy for foodies that conditions you to be non-vegetarian if you want to enjoy them. Hong Kong has a good network of transport like metro trains, buses, tramways, ferries, etc. that makes any corner of the city easily reachable. No need to admit that it is continuously ranked the most expensive city in the world, so don't attempt to convert your expenses into INR else you may not dare to spend for anything here.
